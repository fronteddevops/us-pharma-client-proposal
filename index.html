01 · Overview

Executive Summary

A high-level summary of the platform, its commercial model, and the two-stage delivery approach.

This proposal outlines the design, feasibility, and phased delivery of a multi-tenant pharmacy and peptide marketplace platform. The platform is operated by a single business owner (the Admin) and enables independent providers and clinics to sell pharmacy products and peptides through their own branded storefronts.

Unlike a single-store e-commerce site, this is a business-to-business-to-consumer (B2B2C) platform. The Admin maintains the peptide catalog; pharmacies upload their own product inventory to the platform. Each provider selects products into their own storefront and sets their own retail price — their margin sits on top of the platform's base price. Customers are invited by a specific provider and buy only from that provider's store.

Fulfillment is routed by product type: peptide orders are fulfilled by the platform owner directly, while pharmacy-product orders are redirected to the relevant pharmacy partner who fulfills them. A separate affiliate channel rewards partners for recruiting providers, earning a recurring commission based on those providers' ongoing sales.

Delivery is structured in three stages. Stage 1 — the MVP — is targeted at 45–60 days and delivers a working platform across five roles: Admin, Provider, Store Staff, Customer, and Pharmacy. Stage 2 layers on the payment gateway, automated revenue splits, the affiliate role and commission ledger, full prescription verification, accounting depth, and support tooling. Stage 3 makes the platform AI-first — an AI layer running through every role, from a customer shopping assistant to AI-assisted prescription triage, all built on the platform's own data.

Deployment — Two Options

The client can choose between a managed AWS deployment and a single dedicated VPS. Both run the same application code; they differ in scalability, resilience, and cost.

☁️ Option A — AWS

Recommended

A managed, scalable setup where each part of the platform runs on a service built for it. Resilient, backed up, and able to grow with the platform.

  • ▸ Amazon DocumentDB — managed MongoDB-compatible database
  • ▸ EC2 — runs the Node.js / Express application
  • ▸ S3 + CloudFront — hosts and delivers the React front end
  • ▸ S3 — stores images, files, Rx documents and invoices
  • ▸ Scales each layer independently; automated backups; high availability

🖥️ Option B — Single VPS

Lower Cost

A single dedicated server (for example, Hostinger) running the entire platform — database, application, front end and file storage all on one machine.

  • ▸ Database, Node.js app, React build and files all on one server
  • ▸ Lower and more predictable monthly cost
  • ▸ Simpler to set up and manage initially
  • ▸ Single point of failure — no built-in redundancy
  • ▸ Limited headroom — scaling means a bigger server, not more servers
Recommendation: AWS (Option A) is recommended for a platform that handles healthcare-adjacent data, prescriptions and partner earnings, and is expected to grow as providers and pharmacies are added. The single-VPS option is viable for an early launch or a tight infrastructure budget, but it carries a single point of failure and limited room to scale. The deployment choice can be confirmed at the start of the project and does not affect the application code.
05 · Security & Compliance

Security & Regulatory Considerations

A marketplace handling pharmacy products, prescriptions, and partner earnings carries real security and regulatory weight. The platform is designed with the following safeguards.

ConcernAreaMitigationPriority
Cross-tenant data exposure Platform Every query is scoped by tenant ID at the data layer; role-based access control is enforced server-side, never trusted from the client. Critical
Prescription document handling Compliance Rx files stored encrypted in access-controlled storage, visible only to authorized provider/staff and Admin. Full verification workflow arrives in Stage 2. Critical
Payment & cardholder data Payments Stage 2 — card data is handled by the client-provided gateway's hosted components and never stored on the platform's servers. Critical
Account takeover Auth Hashed credentials, JWT with short expiry, optional two-factor authentication for Admin, Provider and Pharmacy roles. High
Split / commission tampering Accounting Stage 2 — immutable transaction ledger, server-side split calculation, full audit trail on every financial event. High
Regulatory scope (FDA / state pharmacy) Legal Platform provides Rx-gating and audit logs as compliance tooling — but licensing and dispensing legality rest with the client and require legal review. Review
Important regulatory note: Selling peptides and pharmacy products in the US is subject to FDA rules and state pharmacy board regulations, and certain products may require a valid prescription and a licensed dispensing entity. This platform supplies the technical compliance tooling — Rx upload, gating flags, and audit logging — but it is not a substitute for legal counsel. We strongly recommend the client engage a healthcare/pharmacy attorney to confirm which products may be sold, by whom, and under what licensing. The platform can then be configured to enforce those rules.
06 · Scope Assumptions

Confirmed Scope & Assumptions

The plan below is based on the following confirmed decisions. Any change here may adjust scope and the timeline.

  • MVP roles — five live. Admin, Provider, Store Staff, Customer, and Pharmacy go live in the 45–60 day MVP. The Affiliate role is delivered in Stage 2.
  • Payment — manual in MVP, gateway in Stage 2. The MVP launches with order placement only; orders are marked paid manually by Admin or Provider. The client-provided payment gateway and automated revenue splits are added in Stage 2.
  • Rx — simple upload in MVP. Customers can upload a prescription file for Rx-flagged products; a full pharmacist/provider verification workflow is a Stage 2 addition.
  • Split fulfillment. Peptide orders are fulfilled by the platform owner (Admin). Pharmacy-product orders are redirected to the relevant pharmacy partner, who fulfills them.
  • Dual-source catalog. The Admin manages the peptide catalog; pharmacy partners upload their own pharmacy-product inventory to the platform.
  • Affiliate commission — recurring. Affiliates earn a recurring commission tied to the ongoing sales of the providers they recruit, not a one-time signup bounty.
  • Technology — MERN stack. The platform is built with MongoDB, Express, React and Node.js.
  • Deployment — client's choice of two options. AWS (DocumentDB, EC2, S3 + CloudFront — recommended) or a single dedicated VPS such as Hostinger. The choice does not affect application code.
  • Provider tools. Providers can chat with their customers in-app and access training videos and onboarding resources from their dashboard.
  • Notifications. The platform includes email and SMS notifications — order confirmations, status updates, invitations, and account alerts — with templates managed by the Admin.
  • Responsive web only. All interfaces are responsive web applications. A native mobile app is not part of this scope.
  • UI/UX by a third party. Visual design and the component library are supplied by the client's design partner; this estimate covers front-end implementation only.

08 · Feasibility

Feasibility Analysis

A structured assessment confirming the project is viable, well-scoped, and built on proven technology.

⚙️ Technical Feasibility

High

Every component — multi-tenant data models, role-based access, dual-source catalogs, fulfillment routing — is well-established, production-proven technology used across thousands of marketplace platforms. Nothing here is experimental.

⏱️ Timeline Feasibility

Tight but Workable

45–60 days for a 5-role MVP is aggressive. It is achievable specifically because payments, the affiliate role, and Rx verification are deferred to Stage 2. The MVP depends on prompt design delivery and fast review turnaround; delays in either compress an already tight schedule.

⚖️ Regulatory Feasibility

Review Needed

US pharmacy and peptide commerce is regulated federally and by state. The platform enforces compliance rules — Rx gating, audit trails — but the rules must come from the client's legal counsel. A legal review should run in parallel with development.

📈 Scalability Feasibility

Very High

The multi-tenant, containerized architecture supports unlimited provider storefronts and pharmacy partners from a single deployment. Adding a partner is a data operation, not an infrastructure one.

🏪 Operational Feasibility

High

The Admin onboards partners, providers build their own storefronts, pharmacies manage their own stock, and staff handle orders — all through dashboards designed for non-technical users.

🎨 Design Dependency

Conditional

Front-end implementation depends on the third-party UI/UX team delivering designs on schedule. Given the compressed MVP window, designs for each sprint should land at least one week before that sprint begins.
